SCALA Project: Alternative Communication System for the Literacy of People with Autism

The SCALA project emerged in 2009 with the proposal to support the literacy of children with autism. It is an online platform, available at scala.ufrgs.br, which seeks to support the person who does not practice orality. The site, also available in English and Spanish, has one version for the internet and another in the form of a tablet application. SCALA is also being used in the intellectual disability of people with cerebral palsy and with children who have selective mutism. The software can be used in the clinic, at school and even at home.

UFRGS Mapas (iOS)

UFRGS Mapas is an application aimed at the academic community of UFRGS and for visitors. It works with a map that shows the location of any building within UFRGS. Search by location can be made either by name or by the building number.

UFRGS Maps was also developed at CPD / UFRGS. I had the opportunity to rewrite and develop this application from scratch when a web service was created for it. Previously, the application locally maintained a database with all university buildings.

Inventário UFRGS (iOS)

The UFRGS Inventory was created to be the main tool in collecting inventory that happens every 2 years at the university. In addition to making it easy to retrieve information about belongings by reading the assets barcode, it displays information simply and cleanly, making editing easier.

I participated in all stages of application development. From database creation to development and testing. The best part of working with Inventory was the possibility of using different mechanisms of the smartphone, such as reading bar codes by the camera phone.

REALiTi (iOS game)

REALiTi was developed by myself and Lucas Assis during our time at the Apple Developer Academy. This application was our final project and one of the most fun and creative projects I had the opportunity to participate.

REALiTi is a jigsaw puzzle-style game that challenges the player both in assembling the shapes and in the ability to discover hidden stages and hidden worlds in the game screens. At each stage, the form / object mounted by the player appears in the scenario and, depending on the player's interaction with this object, it can unlock new things.
